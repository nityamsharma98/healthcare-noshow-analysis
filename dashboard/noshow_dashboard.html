⚠ ANALYST FLAG — SMS reminder is a confound, not a cause

Raw read: patients who got an SMS appear to miss more often — reminders look harmful.

Why that's wrong: SMS is sent mostly for long-lead appointments (see the average lead days below), which are inherently higher-risk. The lead time drives both. The intervention design therefore layers reminder cadence on a lead-time-aware risk score rather than trusting a single SMS.

Prior behavior predicts future no-shows

Among repeat patients, past no-show history is a strong signal — hence its weight in the risk score.
